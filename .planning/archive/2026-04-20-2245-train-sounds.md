## User Intent
Train Sounds should be a calm sound toy, not a progression game. Players should see a full-screen stylized train scene, tap or click accessible hotspots on the engine and visible cars to hear recorded train sounds, and use left and right arrows to cycle through four curated presets: steam, diesel, electric, and high-speed. Each preset only needs a visible train name, not extra per-train info text, while the game still follows the repo’s standard expectations for keyboard, touch, gamepad, reduced motion, PWA wiring, CC0 attribution, and validation.

### What Worked
- LEG-1, LEG-2, LEG-5, LEG-6 process flow was clean: shell, pure state, input/a11y, and validation gates all landed without integration friction.
- `pnpm test:local` was green at handoff; the smoke spec captured a screenshot; attribution sync ran.
- Buffer source firing was verified live in browser (instrumented `AudioBufferSourceNode.start` count incremented on hotspot tap), and OGGs decode cleanly at 48 kHz mono in both Web Audio and `OfflineAudioContext`.
- Catalog/state structure is sound and extensible for future engine+car remixing.

### What Didn't
1. **Audio reads as silent on real devices (BLOCKER, LEG-3/LEG-4).** Net chain peak ≈ −24 dB before the SFX bus compressor (which only engages above −18 dB). Files themselves measure fine (steam-whistle peak −7.3 dB, RMS −20 dB). Confirmed silent on Firefox/Windows. Root cause: per-sample `gain` values in `games/train-sounds/sample-manifest.ts` (typically 0.5) compound with the shared SFX bus's quiet 0.12 default in `client/audio.ts`, starving the signal.
2. **iPhone 17 portrait hotspots misaligned (LEG-1 CSS / LEG-4 renderer).** Hotspot positions in `public/styles/train-sounds.css` were not validated at the 390×844 checkpoint despite the plan listing it as required. The smoke spec's screenshot caught nothing because no bounds assertion was made.
3. **Train cars and engines do not look distinct (LEG-2 catalog / LEG-1 CSS).** Passenger vs. cargo vs. high-speed cars render too similarly; the high-speed train shows a smoke stack; train tracks render in top-down perspective beneath a side-view train (perspective mismatch).
4. **Scene is too busy (LEG-2 scope).** Five hotspots × multiple cars per preset crowds small viewports.
5. **Reduce-motion overhead is unwanted (scope).** User wants animations removed entirely rather than gated by a reduce-motion toggle.

### Chart Gaps
- **Workshop, visual fidelity:** Plan said "stylized DOM/CSS train scenes" but did not require per-variant structural distinctness, did not declare a single shared scene perspective, and did not cap hotspot density per viewport.
- **Workshop, audio audibility:** LEG-3 acknowledged human listening as a gate but did not require a final-chain loudness probe (run sample through `createSfxBus()` configuration, assert net peak ≥ compressor threshold) before claiming done.
- **Alignment, scope:** "Settings tab containing SFX and reduce-motion toggles" was assumed boilerplate. For a calm sound toy with optional animations, this should have been a clarifying question.
- **Workshop, viewport checkpoints:** Plan listed four viewports but did not require the smoke spec to assert hotspot bounds within the scene container at the smallest one.

- N/A — chart-time prompt was reasonably scoped. Gaps were chart workflow gaps, not user-prompt issues.
